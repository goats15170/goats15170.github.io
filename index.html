Introduction

Our team name is Goats 15170. We are a first year robotics team, competing in the First Tech Challenge.

For a majority of the Robotics experience of our members, we were part of our high schoolâ€™s robotics organizations, and were part of GOATS 10761. Due to visible issues with the management of the school team, and the glaring inefficiencies we faced we decided it would be best for us to dissociate ourselves from our school. One of the biggest factors of our secession would be that our school club was not able to fund our team with money as soon as possible. The second reason was that we had been given at least 30 people to be put on our team, which is a whole lot to control with few leads. Even though this huge change has occurred to our team, we will pull through together through thick and thin, because we see each other a family. From late night robotics meetings at our team lead house to hanging out together in local parks, robotics has been a pivotal part of all of our lives. More than just expanding our social horizons, robotics has managed to become an outlet of expression and proof of concept for all of us. Also, our entire team has taken classes, such as AP Physics and Java, and our robot is the perfect manifestation of our years of working and practicing skills by ourselves. Using that new knowledge we are able to have a better understanding of our robot and we can improve our robot in many ways. And with the help of many skilled mentors, we have been able to gather a worldwide view on what we do in robotics, as well as seeing the practicality and application of all the skills we learn. From trial and error to the importance of prototyping, we have been able to learn the basic building blocks of engineering and teamwork.

Team Branches

Throughout the season, we've focused in numerous branches of engineering and computer science. This, in addition to our outreach efforts, has allowed us to get an access to our personal interests through robotics.

  • Java
  • Electrical Systems
  • Finance
  • Mechanical Systems

We have separate teams focused on each topic, but all of our members work in all areas.

Our Team

Our team is primarily full of seniors and juniors. We have about 2-3 years of FTC experience amongst us.

As a team, we've grown from members of a robotics team into a tight group of friends that spends a majority of their freetime together. Growing to build strong relationships within the team has allowed us to work efficiently (and inefficiently) and have fun during every step.
